Hi! I'm Douwe Hoekstra, a mathematician from the Netherlands, currently pursuing a Master's degree at the Radboud University

Profile

Ever since I was twelve years old, I wanted to become a mathematician.This, together with my broader interest in the natural sciences, formed my primary reason for studying both mathematics and physics. I obtained both BSc’s summa cum laude. Driven by my passion for mathematics, I am continuing my studies with a Master’s degree in mathematics. Simultaneously with this next step in my studies, I started broadening my horizon by taking on the positions of secretary and treasurer within several student organisations over the course of multiple years. This also led to my second passion for (Dutch) law. From a young age, I have been driven by curiosity, a strong sense of justice and my principles. I take responsibility, act independently or discuss/collaborate where necessary and get the job done! Helping others through what I do, and applying my talents and expertise, gives me satisfaction. My short-term goal is to obtain a PhD in mathematics. In the long term, I would like to be a mathematician in academia, contributing to the common body of knowledge.

Faculty of Science Honours programmeAug. 2019 – Sept. 2021
Radboud University, Nijmegen, the Netherlands

This is an extracurricular program for excellent students that seek extra challenges. It takes two years and it is geared towards teaching the skills necessary for a scientific career. In the first year, you have to write a research proposal in a small interdisciplinary group. Our group wrote a proposal on the magneto-reception of robins, combining physics, chemistry and biology.

In the second year, you get extra time to work on your thesis so that you can actually perform research. This extra time allowed me to dive into a active field of research of mathematics and in that way I have proven some new results.

The program is also called "Een machtig avontuur" (a powerful adventure) and it treats topics from the natural sciences from the very small up to the very big.
